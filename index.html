THE LIFE OF RAFI KHAWAR

Nanha, whose real name was Rafi Khawar, was one of the most recognizable faces of Pakistani film and television. Known for his expressive acting and natural comic timing, he carved a unique place for himself in the entertainment industry during the 1960s and 1970s. His performances brought laughter to millions, and his innocent screen persona made him a household name across the country.

Born into a modest family, Nanha developed an interest in acting at a young age. He began his career in theatre before transitioning to films, where he quickly rose to fame. Unlike many comedians of his era, his humor was not forced or exaggerated; instead, it was rooted in simplicity and relatability. Audiences admired him not only for his comic roles but also for the emotional depth he occasionally displayed in serious performances.

Over the years, Nanha appeared in numerous successful films and shared the screen with leading actors and actresses of the time. Directors valued his professionalism and dedication to his craft. Despite his public success, those close to him describe him as a sensitive and deeply thoughtful individual. His sudden death has left a void in the film industry, and fans continue to remember him as the man who made an entire nation smile.

ACTRESS RECALLS FRIENDSHIP

Lahore film industry actress Nazli, expressing her deep sorrow over Nanha's passing, said that she still cannot believe he took his own life.

"I cannot even imagine that he would do such a thing. I simply do not understand why he did this. He should not have done it," she said.

She added that the shock of his death is beyond words and that she is unable to fully express her grief. Nazli further stated that due to the circumstances, she was unable to have a final glimpse of Nanha, a regret she will carry for the rest of her life.

Nanha belonged to a respected and educated family. His father was a doctor and held a prominent position among senior government officials.

Nazli was questioned by the police as part of the investigation.

According to police sources, Nanha was deeply in love with Nazli, but she had recently begun distancing herself from him.

Nanha's wife and son have also been included in the investigation. It was further revealed that this was not the first time he had attempted to end his life; there had reportedly been previous attempts.

INVESTIGATION TIMELINE

  1. Initial report filed by family members at 7:30 AM
  2. Police arrived at the scene at 8:15 AM
  3. Medical examiner confirmed death by gunshot wound
  4. Weapon recovered from the scene
  5. Statements collected from family members and Nazli

According to the Lahore correspondent, a formal inquiry into Nanha's death has been initiated. The case has been registered under Section 174 (proceedings in cases of unnatural death), and legal formalities are underway.

Police have also taken into custody a 22-magazine automatic rifle recovered from Nanha's room as part of the investigation.
